I am primarily interested in teaching on topics related to international relations, international security, civil conflicts, natural disasters, natural resources, foreign aid, political violence, and research methodology.  

My teaching centers around adapability to students' needs and perspectives, changing my approach to lecture and discussion as student needs change to maximize their understanding. By spending time to approach concepts in ways accessible to students, I bridge student learning styles to tie concepts and arguments to contemporary issues, empirical analysis, and real-world applications.
